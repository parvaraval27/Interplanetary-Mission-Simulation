Trade-off Analysis

Tsiolkovsky Rocket Equation

Δv = Isp × g₀ × ln(m₀ / mf)

Δv: Change in velocity (m/s)
Isp: Specific impulse (seconds)
g₀: Standard gravity (9.81 m/s²)
m₀: Initial mass (kg)
mf: Final mass (kg)

Mission Design Tips

  • Higher Isp means more fuel-efficient propulsion
  • Hohmann transfers are most fuel-efficient for circular orbits
  • Electric propulsion offers great efficiency but requires long mission times
  • Chemical rockets provide high thrust for quick missions
  • Solar sails work best for inner solar system missions
